Core Concepts

Meacham argues that democracy is not a guaranteed state, but a fragile experiment dependent on human character. Click each framework to explore the source material and connections.

The Warning: Norms vs. Laws

Meacham emphasizes that "Norms" are doing the right thing when the law doesn't force you to. The crisis occurs when leaders abandon norms and only respect the literal letter of the law to seize power.

The Anthology's Argument

From American Struggle: Meacham frames the book as a chorus of voices (sometimes discordant, always fascinating) showing that conflict is perennial, not aberrant.

Glossary

Semiquincentennial: The 250th anniversary.

Norms: Unwritten rules of conduct that sustain the legal system. Doing the right thing when the law doesn't force you to.

Social Contract: The implicit agreement among citizens to constrain individual desires for collective benefit.

"Better Angels": Lincoln's phrase for humanity's capacity for reason, compassion, and restraint over fear and appetite.
